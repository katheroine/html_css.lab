In Japanese language learning, the word 漢かん字 (kanji) is often accompanied by 読みよみ仮名 (furigana) to indicate pronunciation.

For example, the phrase 先生せんせい (teacher) is written with the reading above the characters, helping learners read correctly.

In historical texts, complex words like 気き息 (breath or air) can be annotated with ruby to clarify pronunciation or meaning.

When teaching children, the ruby element helps present multilingual or special terms clearly, such as Bonjour﻿ボンジュール ("Hello" in French).
